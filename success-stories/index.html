PalawanPay — From Zero to a Scalable Care Organization

PalawanPay launched in 2022 into a crowded PH fintech market. Competing with unicorns meant the bar for customer care wasn’t “answer tickets” — it was build a disciplined, data-driven operation that could scale without chaos.

Use cases

The mandate was simple and brutal: stand up Customer Care from scratch, then scale it past 150 agents without letting data rot or SLAs fall apart.

We needed an omnichannel stack that routed cleanly, a Help Center the team actually used, and governance that kept fields, tags, and macros from turning into spaghetti. Leadership wanted insight without spreadsheet gymnastics, and product teams needed a repeatable way to launch new services without blind-siding operations.

In short, PalawanPay didn’t just need tools — it needed a backbone: queues, schedules, and ownership that would survive growth and constant change.

Our Approach

I built the care organization and the system it runs on — structure first, speed second, automation where it pays.

I designed the operating model, stood up queues and schedules, and put real ownership in place. Zendesk went from zero to production with purpose: forms and required fields mapped to reporting, triggers and automations that respect clock rules, roles and permissions that keep admin safe, Guide for self-service, and Explore for leadership views that make sense at a glance.

On the integration side, I wired the essentials so tickets carried the context agents need — orders, payments, shipping — and I kept governance tight so tags meant something months later. I authored the policies and playbooks the team uses daily, set the KPIs that matter (FRT, AHT, CSAT, backlog health, reopen rate), and enforced a review cadence so performance didn’t drift.

Because startups ship fast, I created a rollout framework for new products: before launch, Care already has routing, SLAs, macros, and dashboards in place. Where automation made sense, we added it — intent tagging, smart replies, summaries — with human-in-the-loop controls to avoid bot stupidity.

Bottom line: org design, platform build, integrations, documentation, vendor management, and performance leadership — one accountable owner to take it from blank slate to reliable daily operation.

Success story

Outcomes are expressed as confidential ranges; the impact was obvious on the floor and in exec reviews.

First-response times tightened by roughly forty to sixty percent on priority queues once routing, SLAs, and schedules were cleaned up. Data quality stopped lying — fewer misroutes and reopens meant managers could trust dashboards again. Onboarding moved faster because playbooks and macros weren’t optional; they were the way the work got done.

The new-product framework cut thrash. Care shipped with the product instead of chasing it, and leadership finally saw one version of the truth in Explore. Annualized savings landed around ₱5M from automation, license/right-sizing, and removing wasted steps — not by squeezing agents, but by making the system less dumb.

This is what “from zero to scale” looks like when you refuse vanity metrics and design for reality.
