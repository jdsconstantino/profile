PalawanPay — From Zero to a Scalable Care Organization

PalawanPay launched in 2022 into a crowded PH fintech market. Competing with unicorns meant the bar for customer care wasn’t “answer tickets” — it was to build a disciplined, data-driven operation that could scale without chaos.

Use Cases

The mandate was simple and brutal: stand up Customer Care from scratch, then scale it past 150 agents without letting SLAs or data integrity fall apart. We needed clean omnichannel routing, a Help Center the team actually used, and governance that kept forms, fields, and macros from turning into spaghetti. Leadership wanted insight without spreadsheet gymnastics, and product needed a repeatable way to launch new services without blindsiding operations.

Our Approach

Build the backbone first, then add speed — and automate where it pays. We designed the operating model, stood up queues and schedules, and put real ownership in place. Zendesk went from zero to production with purpose: forms mapped to reporting, triggers and automations that respect clock rules, roles and permissions that keep admin safe, Guide for self-service, and Explore for leadership views that actually inform decisions.

Integrations brought orders, payments, and shipping context into tickets. We authored the policies and playbooks, set the KPIs that matter (FRT, AHT, CSAT, backlog health, reopen rate), and enforced review cadences. For new products, we created a rollout framework so routing, SLAs, macros, and dashboards ship with the release. Automation covered intent tagging, smart replies, and summaries with human-in-the-loop controls.

Bottom line: org design, platform build, integrations, documentation, vendor management, and performance leadership — one accountable owner from blank slate to daily reliability.

Success Story

First-response times tightened by roughly 40–60% on priority queues once routing, SLAs, and schedules were cleaned up. Data quality stopped lying — fewer misroutes and reopens meant managers could trust dashboards again. Onboarding moved faster because playbooks and macros weren’t optional; they were the way the work got done. Annualized savings landed around ₱5M from automation, license/right-sizing, and removing wasted steps.

US Door & More — From Scattered Answers to an AI-Aware Help Center

High-consideration e-commerce means lots of pre-purchase questions and WISMO churn. Support lived across email threads, CMS notes, and tribal memory, so agents rewrote the same answers and customers waited for basics.

Help Center Knowledge Architecture AI Agent Grounding Deflection Macros & Governance Reporting

Use Cases

Centralize product guidance (sizes, handing, glass options, lead times), tame WISMO volume, and make the AI agent trustworthy. The goal: one place customers and agents both rely on — structured content the bot can cite, and macros that stop ad-hoc replies.

Our Approach

We designed a help center that mirrors how shoppers decide: Plan → Choose → Order → Track → Install → Warranty. Each category used strict templates (Purpose, When to use, Steps, Specs, Edge cases, Links) so answers stay consistent.

Articles were tagged by intent (pre-purchase, order status, installation) and mapped to macros, so agents reply once with a canonical link instead of rewrites. The AI agent was grounded on this corpus and instructed to quote articles verbatim with links, not hallucinate.

We added form-based contact with required fields (order #, door series, glass family) to cut back-and-forth, and Explore views to track search-to-ticket and deflection by intent.

Translation: one knowledge spine, clean macros, AI that answers from source — not vibes.

Success Story

Repeat “one-touch” questions (WISMO, sizing, handing) dropped materially once the AI agent was restricted to the help center and agents linked articles instead of rewriting. First-response quality improved, and leadership finally saw what customers searched before contacting support, which guided the next wave of articles.

Boutique de Sophie — Scaling CX on a Budget Plan (EU Startup)

Sales spiked; tickets, complaints, and disputes followed. The account was on a Support-tier plan, so no fancy enterprise add-ons. We had to ship scale with constraints — automate the boring, surface the risky, and give leadership a live view without buying new tools.

Shopify → Zendesk Dispute Reduction Automation & Macros Multilingual Support Dashboards Governance

Generalized narrative; no live data or private artifacts are shown.

Use Cases

Stabilize CX during a demand surge: cut one-touch noise, prevent avoidable chargebacks, and shorten the dispute cycle. Give agents canonical answers in multiple languages, enforce SLAs, and give leadership a real-time dashboard without upgrading plans.

Our Approach

Wire the context in: used Shopify APIs and available Zendesk endpoints to inject orders, payment status, fulfillment events, and RMA flags directly into tickets. Built a lightweight middleware to proactively create tickets on risk signals (failed capture, carrier exception, refund request keywords) so we control the narrative before it becomes a chargeback.

Make answers instant, in any language: codified macros per intent (WISMO, sizing, returns, refund timing) with translation variants; triggers apply locale based on Shopify shipping country or customer language and auto-tag by intent for reporting.

Enforce flow, not vibes: added form-based contact with required fields (order #, gateway, SKU, reason codes) to kill the back-and-forth. SLA policies by queue; automations escalate aging “financial risk” tickets to a disputes lane with a checklist.

Dashboards without new spend: Apps Script → incremental ticket sync to Sheets → Looker Studio. Views for FRT/AHT/CSAT, refund rate by intent, pre-dispute outreach, and disputes by gateway/carrier. Scheduled snapshots for month-end.

Playbooks that stick: wrote the KPI set, Customer Care SOP, and a Disputes SOP (evidence packs, timelines per processor, response macros, win-rate tracking). Governance kept forms/fields/macros from turning into spaghetti.

Translation: connect the data, pre-empt the problems, standardize the reply, measure the fallout — all on a Support plan.

Success Story

Total disputes trended down by ~25–35% (net ≈ −30%) as pre-dispute outreach and clean evidence packs kicked in. KPIs became visible daily via Looker Studio; agents spent less time copy-pasting thanks to macros/triggers/automations and translation variants. Leadership finally saw where disputes were born (intent → gateway → carrier), and we tuned content to deflect more of them.
